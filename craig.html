Craig Robinson

"Craig was a unicorn, after all--shaped by his unusual name, his odd heritage, his hard-to-pin-down ethnicity, his missing dad, his unique mind" (Obama 117).

  • 1961

    Birth (1961)

    He was born in Hawaii, to a father from Kenya. His father returned there soon after his birth, eventually having more children. Several years later, his mother remarried and they moved to Indonesia.

  • 1971

    Return To Hawaii (1971)

    Barack was sent back to Hawaii for safety and education reasons, and primarily grew up there with his grandparents. The absence of his father (and frequently of his mother) gave him a level of independence, while his multicultural background gave him a unique perspective on society.

  • 1979

    Graduated From Punahou Academy High School (1979)

    Being one of the few black students in a prestigious school, he began to awaken to the realities of racism and inequality.

  • 1983

    Graduated From Columbia University (1983)

    While in college, he began advocating for causes such as protesting Apartheid, and became even more aware of inequality. He majored in political science.

  • 1985

    Began Working As A Community Organizer (1985)

    After college, he worked in business for two years, but then began turning his attention towards improving the world around him. He began this by working in the South Side of Chicago, helping communities push for change from the government

  • 1989

    Michelle And Barack Meet (1989)

  • 1991

    Graduated From Harvard Law (1991)

    While at Harvard, he was elected editor of the Harvard Law Review, which was a high honor not yet bestowed to an African American. After graduating, he began working at Sidley Austin full time.

  • 1992

    Marriage (1992)

  • 1996

    Barack Is Elected To The Chicago Senate (1996)

    This is when Barack began involving himself in politics. This meant that he spent very little time with his family while campaigning and serving in the legislature, but Michelle understood his motivation and supported him. He enacted numerous reform laws.

  • 2004

    Barack Is Elected To The U.S. Senate (2004)

  • 2008

    Barack Is Elected President Of The U.S. (2008)
