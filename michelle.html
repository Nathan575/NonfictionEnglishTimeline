Michelle Obama

"I know that I am who I am not because of the titles I've held or the celebrities I've met, but because of the snaking paths and winding roads, the frustrations and contradictions, the constant growth that is painful and joyful and full of confusion" (Obama xi).

  • 1964

    Birth (January 17, 1964)

    She was born in the South Side of Chicago to a mixed-race lower-middle class community experiencing “white flight”. This meant that over time, white communities began migrating out of her neighborhood and into racially homogenous suburbs, ultimately leading to deterioration of her area and of the public spaces in there. This also meant that she grew up with the first-hand effects of structural racism. She had an older brother Craig. Most of her extended family lived nearby, such as her maternal grandfather, could directly trace his ancestry to slaves.

  • 1981

    Graduated From Whitney Young High School (1981)

    As the first magnet school in Chicago, Whitney Young gave her an opportunity to get a better education than she could in the underfunded schools in her neighborhood, such as her middle school. Though the school had racial quotas, when she went it was predominantly black students, continuing to limit her experiences to interactions with peers in similar situations as herself.

  • 1985

    Graduated From Princeton (1985)

    Following her brother, she went to Princeton University. She was encouraged to reach for an Ivy League school by her parents, who hoped that she could obtain a strong education and be more successful than they ever were. This is in contrast to her grandparents, who had been forced to give up on their dreams. While there, she grappled with the difficulty of being placed in a mostly-white, privileged community for the first time, and feeling like an outsider. She joined several groups to support minorities at the university, and began to consider the issue more closely. She majored in sociology.

  • 1988

    Graduated From Harvard Law, Begin Working At Sidley Austin (1988)

    Her goal in college was simply to become educated and obtain a lucrative job. Thus, once she finished obtaining a Harvard education, she began working at the high-end law firm Sidley Austin. Once more in an almost entirely white environment, she ended up focusing on improving the company's hiring of minorities, such as by helping recruit law students.

  • 1989

    Met Barack Obama (1989)

    She met Barack Obama when he became a summer associate at her law office. She was put in charge of mentoring him, and they quickly began bonding over their personalities and opinions on social issues. They both cared deeply about supporting minorities in empowering themselves to rise above their social and economic restraints.

  • 1991

    Her Father Dies (1991)

    Her father was always a massive influence on her life, in a large part due to his unstoppable determination. Despite being diagnosed with multiple sclerosis and becoming disabled, he still went to work every day to support his children's futures. His death was accelerated or even caused due to his willingness to suppress his pain and continue on without troubling others. This tragedy helped inspire Michelle to do something meaningful with her career. She soon left her job at the law firm and began working for the Chicago government and various nonprofits.

  • 1992

    Michelle And Barack Marry (1992)

  • 1996

    Barack Is Elected To The Chicago Senate (1996)

    This is when Barack began involving himself in politics. This meant that he spent very little time with his family while campaigning and serving in the legislature, but Michelle understood his motivation and supported him. He enacted numerous reform laws.

  • 2004

    Barack Is Elected To The U.S. Senate (2004)

  • 2008

    Barack Is Elected President Of The U.S. (2008)

    He becomes the first Black president, and his campaign promises include ending the war in Iraq, reforming healthcare, and supporting minorities.

  • 2010

    The Affordable Care Act Passes Congress (2010)

    This revolutionary healthcare bill dubbed “Obamacare” made it significantly easier for low-income families to acquire healthcare through raised taxes from wealthy families. It was extremely divisive, but was a symbol of what he hoped to accomplish.

  • 2016

    Trump Is Elected President Of The U.S. (2016)
