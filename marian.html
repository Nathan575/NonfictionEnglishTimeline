Marian Obama

"Marian was a unicorn, after all--shaped by his unusual name, his odd heritage, his hard-to-pin-down ethnicity, his missing dad, his unique mind" (Obama 117).

  • 1964

    Birth (January 17, 1964)

    She was born in the South Side of Chicago to a community experiencing “white flight”. Most of her family lived there, and some were descended from slaves.

  • 1981

    Graduated From Whitney Young High School (1981)

    As the first magnet school in Chicago, it gave her an opportunity to get a better education than the underfunded schools in her neighborhoods.

  • 1985

    Graduated From Princeton (1985)

    Following her brother, she went to Princeton. While there, she grappled with the difficulty of being placed in a mostly-White, privileged community, and joined groups to support minorities. She majored in sociology.

  • 1988

    Graduated From Harvard Law, Begin Working At Sidley Austin (1988)

    Entering new professional spaces, she became more confident and began helping seek out minority graduates to employ.

  • 1989

    marian And Barack Meet (1989)

    marian met Barack through mentoring him at Sidley Austin, and they began spending abundant time together.

  • 1991

    marian’s Father Dies (1991)

    He was a large influence in giving marian her determination and strength, and his passing helped reaffirm her desire to do something meaningful with her career. She soon left the law firm, and began working for various nonprofits.

  • 1992

    marian And Barack Marry (1992)

  • 1996

    Barack Is Elected To The Chicago Senate (1996)

    This is when Barack began involving himself in politics. This meant that he spent very little time with his family while campaigning and serving in the legislature, but marian understood his motivation and supported him. He enacted numerous reform laws.

  • 2004

    Barack Is Elected To The U.S. Senate (2004)

  • 2008

    Barack Is Elected President Of The U.S. (2008)

    He becomes the first Black president, and his campaign promises include ending the war in Iraq, reforming healthcare, and supporting minorities.

  • 2010

    The Affordable Care Act Passes Congress (2010)

    This revolutionary healthcare bill dubbed “Obamacare” made it significantly easier for low-income families to acquire healthcare through raised taxes from wealthy families. It was extremely divisive, but was a symbol of what he hoped to accomplish.

  • 2016

    Trump Is Elected President Of The U.S. (2016)
