Barack Obama

"Barack was a unicorn, after all--shaped by his unusual name, his odd heritage, his hard-to-pin-down ethnicity, his missing dad, his unique mind" (Obama 117).

  • 1961

    Birth (1961)

    He was born in Hawaii to a mother there and a father from Kenya. His father returned to Africa soon after his birth, eventually having more children. Several years later, his mother remarried and they moved to Indonesia. This situation meant that he ended up with siblings and relatives spread across several continents and cultures.

  • 1971

    Return To Hawaii (1971)

    Barack was sent back to Hawaii for safety and education reasons, and primarily grew up there with his grandparents. The absence of his father (and frequently of his mother) gave him a level of independence and self-motivaiton. His multicultural background gave him a unique perspective on society, as well as an array of family members he could turn to for their support and opinions.

  • 1979

    Graduated From Punahou Academy High School (1979)

    He was one of a relatively few number of black students in a fairly prestigious school, and he began to awaken to the realities of racism and inequality. This was the period he described himself as truly beginning to notice the effects of race on society, and question how his multiracial genealogy fits into the world around him.

  • 1983

    Graduated From Columbia University (1983)

    While in college, he began to find his voice by advocating for causes such as protesting Apartheid, and became even more aware of inequality. He majored in political science.

  • 1985

    Began Working As A Community Organizer (1985)

    After college, he worked in business for two years, but then began turning his attention towards improving the world around him. He began this by working in the South Side of Chicago, helping communities push for change from the government. His varied background gave him the ability to connect with people and understand their needs, and his education gave him the ability to actually turn that need into change.

  • 1989

    Met Michelle Robinson (1989)

    He met Michelle Robinson when he became a summer associate at her law office. She was put in charge of mentoring him, and they quickly began bonding over their personalities and opinions on social issues. They both cared deeply about supporting minorities in empowering themselves to rise above their social and economic restraints.

  • 1991

    Graduated From Harvard Law (1991)

    While at Harvard, he was elected editor of the Harvard Law Review, which was a prestigious honor not yet bestowed to an African American. This basically allowed him to work anywhere he wanted, and after graduating he began working at Sidley Austin full time in order to have a stable high-paying job.

  • 1992

    Marriage (1992)

    Michelle had a more traditionalist idea of marriage as a complete union between two people. He, however, had a looser definition, believing that their love was enough. This was influenced by his loose family, with an absent father and a long-distance mother.

  • 1996

    Barack Is Elected To The Chicago Senate (1996)

    This is when Barack began involving himself in politics. This meant that he spent very little time with his family while campaigning and serving in the legislature, but Michelle understood his motivation and supported him. He did this because he sought a much larger platform to create change. Indeed, he enacted numerous reform laws.

  • 2008

    Barack Is Elected President Of The U.S. (2008)

    Once more seeking a larger platform, he became the first Black president of the U.S. His campaign promises included ending the war in Iraq, reforming healthcare, and supporting minorities. This message resonated with the public, and his prior experience with community organizing and his prestigious degree gave him the necessary qualifications to make change.

  • 2010

    The Affordable Care Act Passes (2010)

    The revolutionary healthcare system dubbed “Obamacare” made it significantly easier for low-income families to acquire healthcare, and was funded by raised taxes on wealthy families. It was extremely divisive, but was a symbol of what he hoped to accomplish. It also proved that he could make change.

  • 2016

    Donald Trump Is Elected President Of The U.S. (2016)

    Donald Trump represented a stark contrast to the Obamas. He grew up in a fairly wealthy family with access to his father's lucrative business. He had conservative politics that were far less in favor of social reform than the Obamas' were. Trump also made false claims that Barack was Muslim or born in Africa in an attempt to use racism against him. Trump also did not disavow White supremacists, standing against everything the Obamas were for.
